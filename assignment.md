# Take-home Assignment: AI Builder

**Duration:** 72 hours

## Background

Hiive is a marketplace for pre-IPO stock. The business is operationally complex: small teams manage high volumes of deals, contacts, documents, and communications across multiple desks. The company operates in a regulated environment where both accuracy and speed are critical.

### Key functions at Hiive

- **Sales and Trading** — Sales reps manage hundreds of relationships (buyers and sellers of pre-IPO stock) across dozens of companies. They decide who to contact, what to say, and when.
- **Marketing** — A small team runs campaigns and nurture flows for buy-side and sell-side prospects, aiming to identify what works versus what doesn't.
- **Compliance** — Responsible for reviewing rep communications, running KYC checks, flagging transaction issues against FINRA and SEC requirements, and maintaining audit trails.
- **Transaction Services** — Handles document collection, prepares signature packages, manages ROFR workflows, follows up on missing counterparty information, and oversees deals from agreement to settlement.
- **Finance** — Manages transaction reconciliation, commission tracking, tax filings, and regulatory reporting.

## Your Goal

Pick one function at Hiive and build an AI agent that solves a real problem for that team.

There is no prescribed format and no single right answer. Example ideas include:

- Intake triage agent
- KYC document reviewer
- Deal pipeline monitoring agent (flags risks or missed steps)
- Lead scoring system based on conversion data
- Marketing performance analyzer (signal vs. noise)
- Or a completely new workflow automation

You may build:

- One deep solution (preferred), or
- Multiple smaller solutions

> **Priority:** Depth over breadth. One well-executed solution is better than several incomplete ones.

## Parameters

- Use any AI tools you want (expected).
- The assignment is intentionally difficult without leveraging AI as a builder.
- Aim for something functional — even if rough — that could realistically be used.
- If blocked, make reasonable assumptions, document them, and continue.

## What to Submit

### 1. Your Output(s)

Provide your work in any accessible format (link, file, GitHub repo, etc.).

### 2. Short Write-Up (max 400 words)

Include:

- What problem did you choose and why?
- What you built
- How it works

### 3. One-Paragraph Reflection

- Where did AI help the most?
- Where did it fall short?

### 4. Optional: Coding Session

Attach your coding session (e.g., Cursor, Claude Code, Codex, etc.) to showcase your workflow with AI tools.
